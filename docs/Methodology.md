# Methodology

*This document describes the methodological framework underlying the use of CORE in empirical research. Rather than defining a specific matching algorithm, it explains how computational procedures, scientific judgement, and knowledge preservation are integrated into a transparent and reproducible research methodology.*

---

## Purpose

CORE has been developed as a research methodology for entity resolution rather than as a standalone matching algorithm. Its purpose is to support empirical research by combining computational efficiency with transparent scientific judgement, allowing entity resolution decisions to become explicit, reproducible, and reusable components of the research process.

The methodology recognises that entity resolution extends beyond identifying correspondences between records. It includes data preparation, computational evaluation, expert review, knowledge preservation, and the production of analysis-ready datasets. Each of these activities contributes to the overall scientific validity of empirical research.

Accordingly, CORE provides a methodological framework through which researchers are able to organise, document, evaluate, and preserve entity resolution decisions while maintaining transparency throughout the complete research lifecycle.

---

## Methodological Principles

The methodology implemented within CORE is founded upon five complementary principles.

### Transparency

Every important methodological decision remains observable and open to scientific inspection.

### Scientific Judgement

Computational procedures support expert reasoning but do not replace it whenever uncertainty remains.

### Reproducibility

Entity resolution decisions are documented in a manner that allows independent verification and future replication.

### Knowledge Preservation

Validated scientific judgement is preserved beyond individual projects so that future investigations may benefit from previous empirical evaluation.

### Methodological Continuity

Entity resolution is regarded as a cumulative research activity in which validated knowledge contributes to successive investigations rather than being repeatedly reconstructed.

## Reproducibility

Within CORE, reproducibility extends beyond obtaining identical analytical datasets. The methodology seeks to preserve the reasoning through which entity resolution decisions are established, allowing subsequent researchers to inspect, evaluate, and reproduce the methodological process itself.

Computational procedures contribute reproducibility by applying consistent rules across observations. Scientific judgement contributes reproducibility by remaining documented, transparent, and open to future evaluation rather than becoming an undocumented component of individual research practice.

Reproducibility is therefore understood as the ability to reconstruct both analytical outputs and the methodological pathway through which those outputs were produced.

---

## Scientific Outputs

The primary output of CORE is not limited to an analysis-ready dataset.

The methodology produces multiple forms of scientific output, including validated entity resolution decisions, documented expert judgement, preserved methodological evidence, and reusable scientific knowledge. These outputs complement the analytical dataset by providing transparency regarding how empirical observations have been interpreted and connected.

Consequently, entity resolution becomes a documented research activity whose methodological products remain available for future scientific investigation rather than disappearing at the conclusion of an individual project.

---

## Methodological Perspective

CORE approaches entity resolution as a scientific methodology rather than as a standalone computational task.

The framework integrates computational procedures, scientific judgement, expert review, and knowledge preservation into a coherent methodological system that supports transparent and reproducible empirical research. Each component contributes a distinct responsibility, while together they establish a continuous process through which uncertainty is evaluated, knowledge is validated, and scientific understanding is preserved.

From this perspective, the purpose of entity resolution extends beyond constructing analytical datasets. It becomes a process through which empirical knowledge is generated, documented, preserved, and made available for future research.

The methodology implemented within CORE therefore seeks not only to improve entity resolution, but also to strengthen the continuity, transparency, and cumulative development of empirical research itself.
