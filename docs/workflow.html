Workflow

From Raw Data to Scientific Knowledge

CORE transforms heterogeneous raw data into transparent, reviewable, reproducible, and analysis-ready outputs through a sequence of explicit methodological stages.

Rather than combining multiple operations within a single opaque procedure, each stage performs a distinct scientific responsibility while preserving the intermediate information required to understand how final entity resolution decisions have been obtained.

The workflow therefore represents more than a computational pipeline.

It provides a structured research process in which data preparation, computational evaluation, expert review, knowledge preservation, and output generation remain clearly separated while contributing to a coherent and reproducible methodology.

Each stage produces explicit outputs that become the inputs of the following stage.

Intermediate results remain available throughout the workflow, allowing researchers to inspect, validate, and reproduce every important methodological decision.

Unlike conventional matching pipelines, CORE deliberately preserves uncertainty whenever computational evidence alone cannot support reliable scientific conclusions.

Stage 1 — Data Acquisition

The workflow begins by importing all resources required for entity resolution.

These may include:

  • reference datasets;
  • target datasets;
  • project dictionaries;
  • community dictionaries;
  • configuration files.

At this stage the objective is reproducibility rather than transformation.

Raw observations remain unchanged throughout the workflow.

Stage 2 — Data Preparation

Before entity resolution can begin, observations are standardised to reduce avoidable variation while preserving their scientific meaning.

Typical operations include:

  • text normalisation;
  • tokenisation;
  • suffix preparation;
  • alias preparation;
  • reference preparation.

Importantly, this stage does not attempt to establish correspondences between entities.

Its purpose is solely to improve the quality and consistency of information entering the resolution process.

Stage 3 — Entity Resolution

Entity resolution forms the computational core of the workflow.

Rather than relying upon a single matching algorithm, CORE performs entity resolution through multiple independent methodological operations.

These include:

  • exact matching;
  • suffix normalisation;
  • alias resolution;
  • candidate generation;
  • similarity assessment.

Each component contributes a different form of computational evidence regarding potential correspondences.

The objective of this stage is not maximum automation.

The objective is to resolve observations that can be established confidently while explicitly identifying those requiring further scientific evaluation.

Stage 4 — Expert Validation

Not every correspondence can be established through computational procedures alone.

Observations whose interpretation remains uncertain proceed to expert validation through the Review Queue.

Within CORE, uncertainty is deliberately preserved rather than concealed.

Researchers therefore evaluate:

  • computational evidence;
  • historical information;
  • domain expertise;
  • contextual interpretation;
  • methodological considerations.

Expert validation therefore remains an explicit and transparent stage of the workflow rather than an undocumented correction performed outside the software.

Stage 5 — Knowledge Preservation

Validated decisions generated during expert review represent scientific knowledge that extends beyond the immediate objectives of individual projects.

Rather than allowing this knowledge to disappear once entity resolution has been completed, CORE preserves validated decisions within the Learning Layer.

Knowledge preservation should not be interpreted as autonomous learning.

The framework does not infer scientific knowledge independently.

Instead, it preserves validated expert judgement so that future investigations may benefit from previous scientific evaluation while remaining free to refine, revise, or reject earlier conclusions.

Learning Philosophy

Knowledge preserved within the Learning Layer does not automatically influence future workflows.

CORE currently distinguishes three stages of knowledge integration.

Passive Learning

Validated decisions are preserved but do not influence computational procedures.

Consensus Learning

Knowledge becomes eligible for methodological influence only after sufficient independent scientific validation has accumulated.

Soft Learning

Validated knowledge influences future workflows through transparent score adjustments and review prioritisation rather than automatic acceptance or rejection.

This approach protects future investigations from isolated reviewer errors while preserving cumulative scientific knowledge.

Stage 6 — Analysis-Ready Outputs

The final stage produces datasets suitable for empirical analysis together with the information required to understand how those datasets were constructed.

Typical outputs include:

  • matched datasets;
  • review decisions;
  • learning dictionaries;
  • diagnostics;
  • reproducible metadata.

Researchers therefore obtain not only analytical outputs but also a transparent record of the methodological process supporting those outputs.

Workflow Characteristics

The workflow implemented within CORE has been designed around five methodological characteristics.

Sequential

Each stage builds explicitly upon the outputs of the previous stage.

Transparent

Intermediate outputs remain available throughout the workflow.

Reviewable

Scientific judgement remains an explicit component of the workflow through the Review Queue.

Reproducible

The workflow can be repeated and independently verified across different datasets and projects.

Cumulative

Validated knowledge contributes to successive investigations rather than being repeatedly reconstructed.

Why This Workflow?

Many matching systems focus primarily on producing final datasets.

CORE adopts a different perspective.

The framework seeks to preserve:

  • intermediate reasoning;
  • expert judgement;
  • methodological evidence;
  • scientific continuity.

From this perspective, the workflow itself becomes a scientific contribution rather than merely a computational pipeline.

Final Principle

Entity resolution does not merely produce analytical datasets.

It produces scientific knowledge.

The workflow implemented within CORE has been designed around this principle.
